10-Year Stock Performance Analysis

An end-to-end data project analysing 10 years of equity performance (2014–2024) across AAPL, MSFT, MS, and IBM. Covers feature engineering, data pipeline (Yahoo Finance → SQLite → Tableau), and audience-specific dashboards. A custom Normalized Close metric enables fair, same-scale comparison across all four tickers.

🔎 Results & Findings

Normalised returns rebased to 1.0× at the start of 2014. Final values represent end-of-period multiples on the original investment baseline.

Stock Final Normalised Return Note
MSFT 15.03× ~15× its 2014 value
AAPL 15.00× ~15× its 2014 value
MS 5.77× ~5.8× its 2014 value
IBM 2.12× Clear underperformer
  • AAPL and MSFT are near-tied on raw terminal return (15.00× vs 15.03×) — neither is a clear winner on raw return alone.
  • AAPL’s edge is in consistency: its pre-2020 growth trajectory was smoother, it dominated trading volume throughout the decade (peak annual volumes up to ~60B shares), and showed lower relative drawdowns during the March 2020 COVID crash.
  • MSFT matched AAPL’s terminal return but with higher volatility in specific periods, driven by the Azure cloud re-rating post-2018.
  • IBM was the clear underperformer at 2.12× over 10 years — barely above inflation, a cautionary data point for long-hold equity selection.
  • March 2020 COVID crash is visible as a sharp dip across all tickers, with AAPL and MSFT recovering fastest; IBM’s recovery was notably slower.
  • AAPL trading volume declined over the period (from ~60B in 2014 to ~5–10B by 2024) — a sign of compounding price appreciation, not declining interest.
